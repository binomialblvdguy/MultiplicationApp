# Vedic Shortcut Lessons

### The "Double-Double" (1:2 Ratio)
If the multiplier is **48**, you are really just multiplying by **4** and then doubling the result.
* **Problem:** 57 × 48
* **Step 1:** Double 57 twice (114 → **228**)
* **Step 2:** Double 228 again (**456**)
* **Vedic Form:** 228 // 456
* **Final:** 2736 (228 + 4 | 56)

### The "1-Anchor" Shortcuts
Any multiplier starting or ending in **1** is a "gift." One half of the work is already done!
* **57 × 41** → `228 // 57` (41 is 4:1 ratio)
* **57 × 14** → `57 // 228` (14 is 1:4 ratio)

### Single Digit "Power" Rules (5-9)

**Rule for 5 (The Half-Point):**
* 36 × 5 = **180** (Half of 36 is 18, add 0)
* 37 × 5 = **185** (Half of 36 is 18, add 5)

**Rule for 6 (5 + 1):**
* 37 × 6 → `(37 × 5) // 37`
* `185 // 37` = **222**

**Rule for 9 (The Complement):**
* 37 × 9 → `(37-10) // (100-37)`
* `27 // 63` = **333**

# THE VEDIC TREASURE HUNT

Welcome, Math Detectives! 🕵️‍♂️
Most people see big numbers and get scared. We see big numbers and look for **Secrets**. Here is how to find the "Gifts" and "Golden Pairs" that make multiplication easy.

---

## 💎 THE POWER OF 1 (The Easiest Gift)
If you see a **1** in your number, you basically don't have to do math for that part!
* **12 x 345**: The "1" means the first part of your answer is just **345**.
* **97 x 213**: The "1" in the middle means the middle part of your answer is just **97**.

---

## 👯 THE TWIN RULE (Multiples of 11)
Look for "Twins" like **226**, **322**, or **433**.
If you multiply the first digit, you've already found the answer for the second digit!
* **Example: 443 x 8**
* 4 * 8 = 32. Since the next digit is also a 4, that part is also 32!
* Your Vedic line: **32 / 32 / 24**

---

## 🏆 THE GOLDEN PAIRS (Root vs. Power)
These numbers hide a secret ratio. One part is the **Root** and the other is the **Square**.

### 🟢 ROOT PAIRS (Prefix First)
*The first digit is the small Root. Multiply it first, then square the result!*
* **101** (1² = 1)
* **204** (2² = 4)
* **309** (3² = 9)
* **416** (4² = 16)
* **525** (5² = 25)
* **636** (6² = 36)
* **749** (7² = 49)
* **864** (8² = 64)
* **981** (9² = 81)

### 🔴 POWER PAIRS (Suffix First)
*The last digit is the small Root. Multiply the suffix first, then scale it up!*
* **402** (√4 = 2)
* **903** (√9 = 3)
* **164** (√16 = 4)
* **255** (√25 = 5)
* **366** (√36 = 6)
* **497** (√49 = 7)
* **648** (√64 = 8)
* **819** (√81 = 9)
