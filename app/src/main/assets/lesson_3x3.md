VEDIC RATIOS: THE 3x3 SHIFT
In 3x3 math, the most important step is choosing your CHOP. This determines how many "seats" are in the back of your answer.

THE TRIPLE SLASH RULE (///)
The calculator uses a special code to help you sync your numbers without making a mistake:

The Double Slash (//): Used for a 1-Digit Suffix (Prefix Split).

Example: 12 | 4

Meaning: Only 1 digit can stay in the back seat. Carry everything else!

The Triple Slash (///): Used for a 2-Digit Suffix (Suffix Split).

Example: 1 | 12

Meaning: 2 digits can stay in the back seat. This is your "Life Saver" for big numbers!

THE 3-STEP PROCESS
PICK YOUR CHOP:

Look for the ratio that involves a 1 (like 1:4 or 12:1).

USE THE RATIO:

Solve the side with the "1" first.

Scale that answer to find the other part.

SYNC BY THE SLASHES:

If you see ///, keep the last two digits of your suffix and carry the rest to the prefix.

GOLD MINE PRACTICE
Try these to see the Triple Slash in action:

112 x 112

Split: 1 | 12 (Ratio 1:12)

Sync: Look for the /// to handle the 2-digit carry!

125 x 125

Split: 1 | 25 (Ratio 1:25)

Sync: Two "seats" are protected in the back.

134 x 12 (The Flip)

Flip: 13 | 4 becomes 12 | 14.

Sync: Since the suffix is 14 (2 digits), watch the slash shift!
